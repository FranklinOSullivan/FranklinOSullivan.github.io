Who am I?

I am in my third year of a Bachelor of Engineering (Honors), specialising in Computer systems Engineering. This degree couples electronics and software engineering to teach an array of theoretical and practical skills related largely to computer architecture and embedded systems. Most notably, this includes the teaching of theoretical software development, including object orientated programming, as well as the use of many different data structures and algorithms to solve given problems quickly and efficiently. There is also an emphasis on electronics design. Specifically circuit design, simulation, breadboard testing, PCB development and soldering. Due to this, there has been a focus on considerations such as power used/ wasted, tricks like access to instantaneous current from localised capacitors and the reduction of noise throughout PCB layers.

Learning all of this has allowed me to pursue computer systems design for fun in my own time. Leading to the development of many of my own projects across all three disciplines of electronics, computer systems and software.

What am I looking for?

I would love to work in firmware or embedded systems development. This is the part of the degree that I find the most enjoyable and have the most experience in. The responsibility of working on cyber-physical systems requiring real-time, safety-critical systems would also be a massive bonus as it would teach me many industry skills that are not quickly learned in university or through research.

I plan to gain a position as an intern in which I will be able to do real work for a company while gaining support from senior developers who can help me solve problems efficiently and in the correct manner. It would also be a massive bonus if this position allowed me to return for subsequent periods, allowing my skills to develop further in a familiar environment.
