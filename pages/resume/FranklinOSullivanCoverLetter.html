Since a young age, I have had a great interest in software development and electronics design. What do I know?

The Computer Systems Engineering degree couples electronics and software engineering to teach an array of theoretical and practical skills related largely to computer architecture and embedded systems. Most notably, this includes teaching theoretical software development, including object-orientated programming, and using many different data structures and algorithms to solve given problems quickly and efficiently. There is also an emphasis on understanding basic electronics design, specifically circuit design, simulation, breadboard testing, PCB development and soldering. Along with this, there has been a focus on considerations such as power used/ wasted, design tricks like allowing easy access to instantaneous current from localised capacitors and reducing noise throughout PCB layers.
